Standard Operating Procedure 01

Site Manager Daily Rhythm

Get across every active job, keep Buildern updated, and keep Alex ahead of the next move. The quick guide below is the bit to follow every day. The detail sections further down are there when you need a reminder.

Quick Guide

Every day, in this order

Don't overthink it. Six steps. Run through them daily and the rest of the system holds itself together.

Daily Run Sheet

Use this every day

Print it, screenshot it, whatever works.

  1. Plan the day Before starting, or the afternoon before. Decide where the time needs to go.
  2. Visit every active job Even if one site only needs a quick check. Every job gets eyes on it.
  3. Walk the whole site Progress, current work, quality, compliance, safety, incomplete work, materials.
  4. Update Buildern No visit is complete until the site diary is done for that project.
  5. Send Alex one clean summary One message at the end of the day. Include the material list.
  6. Escalate the big stuff Cost, program, client expectations, safety, compliance, quality, or reputation.
The Non-Negotiables

The simple standard

Three things hold this together. Everything else is detail.

01

Every job gets eyes on it

Time does not need to be split evenly. Some jobs need an hour, some need most of the day. But every active job gets visited and assessed.

02

Use judgement, not guesswork

Before the day starts, look at trades, inspections, deliveries, risk, and activity. Then decide where the time needs to go.

03

Buildern is the record

Texts and memory are not enough. The Buildern site diary needs progress, issues, materials, photos, and follow-ups for every project.

Need More Detail?

Tap a section to open it

The quick guide above covers most of it. The sections below are here for when something is unclear or new.

AHow to plan the day +

Take a few minutes first thing, or the afternoon before, and decide how much time each active job needs.

  • List the active jobs.
  • Work out the order you will visit them.
  • Give more time to jobs with more trades, risk, inspections, decisions, or activity.
  • Give less time to jobs that only need a quick walkthrough.
  • Keep time at the end for Buildern notes and the message to Alex.

Example: Monash might need one hour. Chapman might need six hours. That is fine. The key is that both get checked.

BWhat to check at each site +

Do a full site walk before getting pulled into too many conversations.

  • Progress completed since the last visit.
  • Work happening now.
  • Quality of workmanship and finish.
  • Compliance with drawings, engineering, selections, and standards.
  • Safety risks and housekeeping.
  • Incomplete work or delays.
  • Materials needed to keep progress moving.
CQuality check +

This is not just about whether the work is done. It needs to be done properly and to Rentoule Projects standard.

  • Workmanship.
  • Straightness, levels, falls, set-outs, and spacing.
  • Finish quality.
  • Damage to completed work.
  • Poor trade handovers.
  • Anything rough, rushed, or likely to cause rework.

If something does not look right, document it. Better to pick it up early than pay for it later.

DBuildern site diary +

No site visit is complete until Buildern has been updated for that project.

  • Progress completed.
  • Work currently happening.
  • Trades on site.
  • Quality issues.
  • Compliance issues.
  • Safety risks.
  • Incomplete work.
  • Materials needed.
  • Photos of progress or issues.
  • Instructions given on site.
  • Anything Alex, Jeff, or a trade needs to follow up.
EPhotos to take +

Take photos that help tell the story. A few clear photos are better than none.

  • Progress made.
  • Work before it gets covered.
  • Defects or quality issues.
  • Safety risks.
  • Materials running low.
  • Areas ready for the next trade.
  • Anything that may need proof later.
FEnd-of-day message to Alex +

After all sites have been checked, send Alex one clear update. Don't flood him with scattered messages unless something is urgent.

GWhen to message Alex straight away +

Most things can wait for the end-of-day summary. Message Alex immediately only if:

  • Work will stop today without a material.
  • A trade cannot continue.
  • There is a serious safety issue.
  • There is a serious compliance issue.
  • A delivery has failed or is wrong.
  • A decision is needed before work can continue.
Urgent one for [Project]. [Trade] cannot continue without [material]. Needed by [time/date]. I'll add it to the full material list later, but this one needs action now.
HWhen to escalate to Jeff +

Most issues go through Alex first. Escalate to Jeff when it affects:

  • Cost.
  • Program.
  • Client expectations.
  • Safety.
  • Compliance.
  • Quality standards.
  • Trade performance.
  • Rentoule Projects' reputation.

If you are unsure, flag it. Better early than late.

Final Reminder

The aim is consistency, not perfection

Do this every day and the jobs will feel more controlled, the team will have better information, and problems will get picked up before they turn into dramas.
